# Pad Thai

Thailand's national dish — sweet, sour, salty and umami in one wok.

## Ingredients (2 servings)

- 200 g flat rice noodles (3-5 mm wide)
- 200 g shrimp or chicken breast (sliced)
- 2 eggs
- 100 g firm tofu (cubed)
- 2 spring onions (sliced)
- 50 g bean sprouts
- 50 g roasted peanuts (chopped)
- 2 cloves garlic (minced)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil

### Pad Thai Sauce
- 3 tbsp fish sauce (nam pla)
- 2 tbsp tamarind paste
- 2 tbsp palm sugar or brown sugar
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp chili flakes (optional)

### Garnish
- Lime wedges
- Fresh cilantro
- Extra peanuts

## Instructions

1. **Soak noodles:** Soak rice noodles in lukewarm water for 20-30 minutes until pliable. Drain.

2. **Mix sauce:** Combine fish sauce, tamarind paste, sugar and rice vinegar. Stir until sugar dissolves.

3. **Cook protein:** Heat oil in a wok on high. Fry tofu until golden, remove. Sear shrimp/chicken 2-3 minutes, remove.

4. **Eggs and garlic:** Fry garlic 30 seconds, add eggs and scramble loosely.

5. **Combine:** Add noodles to wok, pour sauce over. Stir-fry 2-3 minutes. Add tofu, protein, spring onions and bean sprouts, toss to combine.

6. **Serve:** Plate up, top with peanuts, cilantro and lime wedges.

## Tips

- Get the wok screaming hot — that's the secret
- Don't overcrowd the noodles or they'll clump
- Tamarind paste is in any Asian grocery store; substitute: 1 tbsp lime juice + 1 tbsp sugar
- Vegetarian: Skip the shrimp, add more tofu and veggies (bell pepper, carrots)

## Nutrition (per serving)
- Calories: ~550 kcal
- Prep time: 30 minutes
- Difficulty: Medium
